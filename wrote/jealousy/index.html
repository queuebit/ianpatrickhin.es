Jealousy

Conventional wisdom has always been that men have it so easy during pregnancy. Of course, that’s true: women do all the physical suffering, and, of course, have to go through labor! But there are certainly elements of being an expectant father that I am finding to be frustrating (much to my surprise). The one that stands out the most has been the jealousy that I’ve been feeling towards the relationship that Christy already has to the baby.

I know, this sounds kind of cooky, but I seriously have found myself feeling envious of her at times, particularly when she tells me she feels the baby kick. See, right now she can apparently feel the baby kicking / moving internally. I think that’s great, and I’m really excited every time she tells me (I mean, how cool is that!?). But the frustrating thing about it is that it’s not the kind of “feeling the baby kick” that you imagine when you hear the phrase: she can only feel it internally, which means I don’t get to share that experience one bit.

In my mind I know that eventually I’ll be able to feel the kicks on the outside, and that (of course) I’ll get more than enough of the baby’s personality over the next several decades, but right now I can’t help but feel like I’m left out of some important bonding experience that Christy is having. The experiences that a father has during pregnancy are very much 2nd-person, and sometimes I think that’s a bit sad.

Again, I know that it’s not all a bed of roses for her (quite the opposite). I don’t have to deal with morning sickness, cramps, aches, pains, fleeting cravings and an inconsistent appetite. But I would take those things if I could, even just for a moment, experience some of the fun parts of being pregnant.

Or maybe this is all me, a guy, living in my dream world and not understanding how god-awful frustrating being pregnant really is. Maybe ignorance is bliss. Maybe, but I don’t know. And I’d like to.
