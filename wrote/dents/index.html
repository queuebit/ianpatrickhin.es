Dents

I opened MarsEdit with the intention of writing about how much I agree with Shawn Blanc’s article about the iPhone 5 being — first and foremost — a tool. But that’s not really what struck me most about his article, so I’m not going to do that.

In an example of truly excellent writing, Shawn’s reflection on the utility of his iPhone is centered on a little personal moment in which he scratched his iPhone 5 (GASP!) while snapping a photograph of his son. As a dad, I can relate to that; it made the story personally relevant to me. In particular, it reminded me of this scene from Cars 2 — one of my son’s favorite films:

Mater: Oh, for a second there I thought you was trying to fix my dents.

Holley Shiftwell: Yes, I was.

Mater: Well then, no thank you. I don’t get them dents buffed, pulled, filled or painted by nobody. They way too valuble.

Holley Shiftwell: Your dents are valuble? Really?

Mater: I come by each one of ‘em with my best friend Lightning McQueen. I don’t fix these. I wanna remember these dents forever.

As a father, it’s not uncommon for my children to break something I value accidentally. My son has torn old books that I’ve had for decades, broken antique toys my grandfather gave to me as a child and — yes — damaged my prized electronics.

But in the end, the memory of that shared moment is far, far more important to me than the condition of that object. My dents are valuable. I wouldn’t trade a room full of mint condition iPhones for a single photo of my son snuggling his mother.
