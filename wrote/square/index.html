Square

I can’t say enough about how exciting I am about Square. This video does an exceptional job of explaining what Square is from the perspective of their ideal customer: a simple, straightforward, always accessible easy payment option for businesses and individuals.

They’re clearly marketing it directly toward the tech-savvy consumer market—person to person transactions like those that result from CraigsList.org—more heavily than the business market, though ultimately I have to believe that both are going to be heavily courted after launch. Those of you who have been following @jack the last several weeks will not that when he demoes Square it is almost always in the context of a small business; that should serve, I believe, as a clue as to Square’s true target market.

I am still convinced that there is more potential to Square than meets the eye, and in my mind it’s a good more. If it’s rolled out properly it could solidify itself as the number-one transaction processing tool on the market, quickly & simultaneously entering the individual, small business, and retail markets. If it continues to be perceived as cool & trendy as it is pre-launch everyone will want one: it will become ubiquitous with credit card transactions. After all, a lot of it’s coolest features are dependent upon the purchaser having a pre-existing Square account.

If it isn’t obvious, I’m excited to try it for myself.

Update (02/13/2010): The good folks at Square were kind enough to invite me to participate in the pilot. I signed up for my account today, and will be sharing my experience here early this week.
