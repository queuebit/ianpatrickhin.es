Chris Bowler on Pocket, Pinbox and the need for a cloud-based Everything Bucket

Chris Bowler — who you likely know as one of the co-founders of Fusion Ads — offers what I think is some of the most thoughtful commentary on the recent release of Pocket. He’s clearly given it a lot of thought, and I think that he and I are basically on the same page with regard to the need for a cloud-based “everything bucket” / inbox.

Where I agree with him most is on the potential utility of Pocket, which I’ve already adopted in lieu of Instapaper and Readability:

What excited me about the news was not the monetizing strategy or the overall execution of the design, although I’m not yet sold on the former and the latter was well done. Rather, I was very impressed with the focus on the save-anything-for-later concept. This is an idea that has been on my mind the past year, as there has been a gap in the services available to us.

For my part, I’ve found Pocket to be useful for exactly this reason: it’s a place that I can throw anything that I find on the fly. Then, at regular intervals — as with my OmniFocus & email inboxes — I can go through and decide what to do with the stuff I’ve thrown in there.

Where we disagree (though, I think it was more of a miscommunication than a disagreement) is with regard to Pocket’s target market. When it was first released, I said the folks at Pocket were “clearly marketing to women — not design / internet geeks.” I think this remark was misunderstood.

Everything about Pocket — the feel of the design, the color choices, the fonts — exudes a “mass-market audience” vibe (to me). Especially when contrasted with something like Instapaper, which admittedly was built by a developer, not a designer, and feels more like a tool than a product, Pocket’s polish indicates to me that are aiming at skipping the early-adopter market (which they assume will come along, anyway, as Chris and I have) and aiming straight for the early-mass market demographic.

I don’t consider this to be a criticism. I think it’s fantastic. My original tweet was meant as a compliment: I was trying to say that they “get it” when it comes to marketing a product for wide adoption. I think Chris may have misunderstood and thought that I meant that Pocket wasn’t up-to-snuff for power users. It is. But it’s also simple, accessible and appealing to a less aggressive and tech-savvy audience — to people who don’t think about “everything buckets” — and that’s what makes it great.

Of course, interesting as the back-and-forth about Pocket’s target market, etc., is to discuss, it’s decidedly tangential in Chris’s post. I’d really encourage you to read what he has to say about his abandoned idea for Pinbox, and why a well-made, cloud based “everything bucket” is a real need in today’s content-heavy world. It’s great stuff from a really thoughtful guy.
