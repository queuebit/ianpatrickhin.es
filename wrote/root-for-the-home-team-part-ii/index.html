Root For The Home Team, Part II

Last month, I wrote a blog post about how living in Los Angeles has made me even more of an Orioles fan. And it has. I love my hometown, and I love my hometown team. But I also have a new home, now, and a new home team. I’m referring — of course — to the Los Angeles Dodgers.

Today, I did something that I would have been unable to imagine a year ago. I bought a Dodgers hat. 

I’ve been fascinated with the Dodgers since I learned that I would be moving to Los Angeles. It’s hard to say what it is, exactly, that’s drawn me to them. They are one of the most storied franchises in all of baseball. They’re iconic. And what’s more, coming from the East Coast I had little pre-conceived bias for or against them. They were just a team — and now they were my local team.

One of the things that struck me when I moved to LA was how often I saw people wearing Dodgers gear. There’s clearly a real sense of local pride in the franchise in this town, which is interesting because — prior to moving here — I had never really understood it to be a “baseball town.” But it is. There’re Dodgers fans everywhere.

At times, it feels like the very fabric of the city is woven in Dodger blue: to be part of Los Angeles is to love the Dodgers. And so perhaps it’s not surprising that as this city has grown on me, so too has it’s team.

Recently, I’ve realized that I am actively following the Dodgers remarkable season. I am rooting for them to do well — albeit quietly and alongside the Orioles. (That’s one of the beautiful things about baseball: I can simultaneously hope for the Orioles and the Dodgers to play well. They’re in different leagues, and their fortunes are not directly intertwined except in rare circumstances.) I’m genuinely stoked that the Dodgers will be going to the playoffs. In fact, I’m really hoping for a Dodgers vs. Red Sox World Series.

They say that home is where the heart is, and my heart has been moving westward. In my short time here, Los Angeles has been very good to me. It’s given me a fresh start, a job that I love, and a wonderful environment to raise my children. Last year, when everything seemed to be falling through, this city was there for me with open arms. And I finally feel ready to embrace it.
