Community

Yesterday, when writing about being a creator, I said:

I want people to know me for the value I add to my community, not for what I’m interested in.

That’s a pretty great line, right? But without defining what I mean by ‘community’ it’s essentially meaningless. So lets dig into that.

A community is any group of people of which you are a part. So for starters, I should have said “communities” — since I am a part of several. Some of my communities are tangible groups of discrete people that I know, personally. Others are more abstract, like the larger Catholic faith community or society at large. The most obvious — and most important — one is my family. (If I’m not adding value to the lives of my family members, not much else matters.)

I feel pretty strongly that any project I undertake should have a clear purpose. In the context of creating things of value to my community, this means answering the following questions:

  • To what community will this project add value?;
  • How will it add value?; and
  • How will I know it has been successful?

My goal is to always add more value than I draw from my communities: to be a net creator of value. And if I do that — give of myself and my talents relentlessly — those communities of people will naturally grow around me and enrich my life.
