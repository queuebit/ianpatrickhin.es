Impostor Syndrome

Today, I was certified as a NationBuilder Architect. I don’t want to get all sentimental, but it really means a lot to me.

I’ve been designing websites for more than ten years, but always as a hobby. For whatever reason, I’ve spent years convincing myself that I’m not a “real” web designer. But, of course, that’s ridiculous. 

I have built themes for Movable Type, WordPress, Expression Engine, Tumblr, and NationBuilder. I code by hand, and I though I’m not always as orthodox about it as I’d like I’m a big believer in web standards. 

But impostor syndrome runs deep, and by its very nature makes you indifferent to your actual skills and accomplishments. And so for years I’ve convinced myself that I am not really qualified to design websites for other people — even though it was a patently ridiculous thing to believe. 

You’re probably thinking: “What on Earth is he doing, writing this on his blog?! Isn’t he supposed to be conveying competency and skill to prospective clients.“ And of course you’re right. But that’s just the thing: I am quite competent, and I can count on one hand the number of people with more experience with NationBuilder than I have. But, because I am human, I haven’t always believed that about myself. I’m sure you can relate.

The truth is that I’m writing about this for the benefit of others — for those people who may be reading it as they are grappling with their own self doubt about whether or not they can follow their own dreams. Let me tell you this: you can, and you’ll be happier for it. 

Life is about focus. Dream big dreams. Do what you love, and love what you do. And above all, know that you are almost certainly far better at the things you love than you — yourself — believe that you are.
