Catching Up

I’ve had a busy few months. Let me catch you up.

Quitting My Job

A few months ago I left my job with the Maryland Department of Human Resources without knowing what I would do next. It was — possibly — the riskiest thing that I have ever done as an adult: impulsive, rash and incredibly liberating.

Why did I quit? As with anything, the reasons are complicated and many, but suffice it to say that it just wasn’t a good fit for me anymore.

I’m good at working in government — which is why I’ve done it for the past four years, and why I got a Master of Public Administration degree — but I don’t love it. In my experience, working in government often feels like treading water: you’re constantly working, but you’re really only addressing the symptoms of the problem (i.e., keeping yourself afloat) rather than progressing towards a solution (i.e., swimming to shore). And that can be incredibly frustrating.

I have realized that I want my work to be truly meaningful, and not just on a personal level; I want to work on things that are going leave a massive mark on the world — things that are disruptive.

Which is what brought me to NationBuilder.

Joining NationBuilder

One of the most compelling things about the internet is its potential to empower, connect and amplify groups and individuals working towards a common goal. But to do that, you need a killer website and a powerful people database — and until recently, there were only two ways to get those things: (1) hack together disperate tools into a buggy, Frankenstein’s Monster-type toolkit or (2) invest massive sums of money into developing a bespoke system. In practice, this has meant that only two sorts of people were able to leverage the Internet’s potential: geeks and the super-wealthy / connected.

NationBuilder’s mission — in a nutshell — is to disrupt that model by offering an insanely powerful, integrated and affordable toolkit specifically designed to organize people around a common cause. We believe at a core level that everyone has the potential to be a leader and are making the best toolkit for community organizing in human history available to everyone.

For my part, I’m a Political Organizer — which means that I’m specifically charged with reaching out to and building relationships in the political community. I’m working with people across the political spectrum — trying to understand their goals, objectives and passions — and help explore whether NationBuilder can help to achieve them. And the best part is that, by genuinely helping them to be successful, I am helping NationBuilder to be successful as well. It’s an incredible business model, and I’m thrilled to be a part of it.

Moving to Los Angeles

The one hard part about this transition for me has been relocating to Los Angeles. Anyone who knows me will know that I am a passionate and enthusiastic Marylander, so moving three-thousand miles from home has been a major adjustment. They don’t know how to make a proper crab cake here; they sing the National Anthem wrong; etc.

I flew to California the night before Election Day all on my lonesome — leaving the wife and kids — and will be here well into December before I’m able to move them out here. It’s probably worth saying that I genuinely don’t know how I’d have done this without FaceTime; thank you, Apple, for letting me see my children every day.

I’ve been here just over a week, now, and everyone has been fantastic. This city — and it’s weather — are nothing short of amazing, and I’m sure I’ll feel right at home just as soon as my family gets here. Who knows, I might even buy a Dodgers hat.

Rebooting The Blog

In the spirit of dogfooding, I have migrated my blog entirely from WordPress to NationBuilder.

With this move, which (nearly) coincides with the new year, I’m going to try mapping out a content strategy for blog posts over the next few months. I’ve never really done this before, but I’m increasingly of the mind that I need to be more intentional about sharing what I know in a meaningful way, rather than just droning on about some random topic from time to time.

So if you have anything particular topics you’d like me to discuss, please let me know on the suggestions page. I’m going to try and compile a decent list in the coming days and use it to map out content for next year.
