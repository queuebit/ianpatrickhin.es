In Defense of Belt Clips

A few weeks ago there was some talk around the Internet about the geekiness of belt clips. Adam Lisagor and Ben Brooks both posted pieces about the cell phone belt clip being the “pocket protector of our generation.” Well, maybe it is. But I just don’t care.

I wear a belt clip. Sometimes I wear two. I carry an iPhone for my personal use and a BlackBerry Curve for work. When you carry two phones like that and you’re in and out of them every few minutes it really helps to have them easily reachable on your belt.

Thanks to my belt clip I always know where my phone is. I have muscle memory to grab it quickly. When one of them goes off—I have them set to vibrate—I know whether I need to answer it right away or ignore it because I can tell which of them it is without looking.

I don’t like to carry two phones, and I try to condense it down to one when I can. I have Exchange (my work email) set up on both phones and I forward calls from the BlackBerry to my personal on weekends. That cuts down on some of the bulk, but it’s the exception, not the rule. And for those times when I need to have them both the belt clips are a real necessity.

And besides, it’s more comfortable. I often wear dress pants, or loosely fit pants, and in pants like that the iPhone, in particular, tends to sit awkwardly and flap against your leg. It’s very uncomfortable and very unsightly. Frankly—as geeky looking as the belt clip might be—I think it’s far less geeky and far more fashionable to have a relatively unobtrusive phone on your belt than to ruin the fit of your dress clothes.

I guess the bottom line for me is that the belt clip has always been much more about utility than it has been about fashion. I’m married. I’m not exactly the coolest guy on the block. And I really don’t care as much about fashion as I care that I’ve got my tools where I need them.

I tend to think about my iPhone like a carpenter thinks about his hammer: I carry it everywhere because I never know when I’m going to need it, and when I need it I need it “right now.” The belt clip serves that purpose in my daily arsenal. Fashion be damned.
