Storing URLs for Later

Earlier today, my friend David Caolo wrote a bit about his difficulty finding an elegant solution for storing the links he finds throughout the day:

I don’t mean articles to read. Instapaper and Pocket handle that task fine. The links depicted above represent videos to watch, products to consider buying and research. Dragging them into the OS X Dock is easy, and it keeps them in front of me, reminding me that they need attention. However, the identical, spring-loaded icons give no indication of where they’re pointing.

Dave… I don’t mean this to be rude, but you’re doing it wrong.

If I could make a suggestion to you, it’d be this: stop using Pocket to store articles to read. Use Instapaper for that.

Instead, use Pocket as your short-term everything bucket. Set it as your “Read Later” service in Tweetbot, install the browser bookmarklet and just start tossing things into it. Don’t think of it as an app for reading things; instead, think of it as another digital inbox that has no other purpose than to hold the internet’s miscellany until you decide what to do with it: archive it for reference, create a new task in OmniFocus or throw it away.

In fact, it’s pretty much tailor made for that very purpose. On the iPhone, the app even comes pre-built with a “Send to OmniFocus” action in the share menu. It’s the perfect bucket for all the random stuff you stumble across in a day spent on the web… and you can add to it from nearly anywhere. Using it as a reading app is just plain wasteful — especially when you have Instapaper for that.

So do yourself a favor and give it a try for a week. You won’t be disappointed.
