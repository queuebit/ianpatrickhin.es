“The Natural Law Scam”

Writing for Religion News, Mark Silk argues that the Natural Law is bogus: a fiction created merely to rationalize an arbitrary argument. He is mistaken.

In fact, the Catechism of the Catholic Church states:

The natural law is immutable and permanent throughout the variations of history; it subsists under the flux of ideas and customs and supports their progress. The rules that express it remain substantially valid. Even when it is rejected in its very principles, it cannot be destroyed or removed from the heart of man. (1958)

If Mr. Silk believes that Cardinal George is misunderstanding the Natural Law, that would be one thing. (Though I would be interested to hear his argument as to why he — as a layperson — is better qualified to interpret it than a Cardinal.) But to assert that it is a mere “rhetorical gun” that “needs to be spiked” is a shaky argument, at best.
