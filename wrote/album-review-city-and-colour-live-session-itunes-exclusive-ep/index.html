Album Review: City and Colour — Live Session (iTunes Exclusive) EP

City and Colour has very quickly become one of my favorite artists. A stage name for Dallas Green, the lead guitarist and vocalist for Alexisonfire, City and Colour is an exceptional example of an artist allowing their inner-self to break out in a side project, only to produce music that is more compelling than that of their “better known” band.

I first heard City and Colour when I saw him as the opening act for Tegan & Saraat Rams Head Live! in Baltimore. I was there, like most everyone else, to see Tegan & Sara, and was skeptical when I saw that the opening act was just a lone man with a guitar. He walked onto the stage rather awkwardly, wearing a pair of well-worn jeans and a lumberjack-style flannel shirt, and carrying nothing but his guitar; he was, quite possibly, the least presumptuous professional musician I have ever seen.

When he started playing I was immediately impressed, which is saying a lot: I’m a very, very jaded concert-goer (not easily impressed). The vocals were spot-on, the music was just the right combination of folk and pop, and there was something about his body-language that just exuded authenticity.

After the concert I went home and immediately downloaded his most recent album, Bring Me Your Love, which, at the time, was the first time I’d ever been so impressed by an opening act to buy their album. I’ve never looked back since; as of today there are 659 plays of City and Colour logged on my Last.fm account.

Now, I said this would be a review of the album “Live Session (iTunes Exclusive)EP,” and it is. If you want an in-home duplication of the experience I described above… listen to this live album. As an alternative, check him out on MySpace Transmissions, which includes video. Whatever you do, please: Do yourself a favor and check him out. You won’t regret it.

Full-Length tracks available for streaming via Last.fm.
