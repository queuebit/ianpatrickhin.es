Fixed Gear Bikes

Ever since I knew what they were I’ve been a fan of fixed gear bikes. There’s is just something about their simplicity that attracts me to them—the clean geometry, the simple maintenance, and the purity of their operation—in a way that’s hard to describe. Fixies always feel like more than just a bike, which sounds a bit silly when written down.

There is something different about riding a fixie that is hard to describe. I think that’s because you aren’t really riding it in the way that you ride a free-wheel bike. On a fixie you almost absorb the bike as an extension of yourself; as you ride it you feel almost like there is no bike at all because you’re just as involved in the process as if you were walking or jogging. It truly is one of those experiences that irrevocably changes your perspective, to the extent that I can’t imagine riding a free-wheeled bike on a regular basis anymore.

I should note that I am in no way a part of the stereotypical “hipster” fixie community. I’m actually more of a yuppie, for lack of a better and less derogatoryterm. I don’t have a single friend who owns a fixie, and don’t know how to do a single trick. I just ride the bike to get me where I want to go. I just like these bikes.

I assembled my first fixie by hand, having collected all of the parts from various sources over several months. It was an incredibly gratifying experience. Every time I got on the bike I felt this incredible sense of ownership that’s hard to describe (are you sensing a theme, here?)—it wasn’t the nicest bike around, but it was my bike. Unfortunately, it was stolen in a burglary in September of 2008 and I’ve yet to replace it.

Happily, however, I think that’s about to change. Starting as soon as this week I plan to start collecting the parts to rebuild it. I had previously considered building a different bike, i.e. taking the opportunity to move in a different direction… to try something new. But after doing a bit of shopping the last few days I think I’m just going to replace what I had, albeit with a bit of an upgrade here and there. The two that jump out in my mind are a Brooks saddle and some Sugino 75s. I’m really pretty excited about it.

So, in closing, lets summarize: (1) I really like fixed gear bikes, (2) mine was stolenout of my living room, and (3) I’m going to start building a carbon copy. I’m quite excited.
