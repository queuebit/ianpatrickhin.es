Christmas 2009

Christmas is an incredibly complex holiday. It is simultaneously about faith & family. Its multiple personalities do not always blend well, and they rarely make scheduling easy. I’d like to share a little bit about my holiday with you, from the lens of each way that I celebrated it.

Mass

The first, and most important, aspect of Christmas is Christ—the Man who made it all happen. We celebrated Mass at our regular parish—The Basilica of the National Shrine of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary—on Christmas Eve at an abnormally early 4:00pm. The Basilica was in excellent form, with Christmas trees & poinsettias arranged ‘just so,’ almost everyone in their Sunday Best, and the choir pulling out all of the stops. Mass was celebrated by Archbishop Edwin O’Brien, and the Gospel was my favorite Christmas reading: Luke 2.

Before we went to Mass it really didn’t feel like Christmas, but afterwards I felt fully charged and ready to embrace the spirit of the season. I suppose that’s as it should be, but it really made an impression on me how much the Mass affected my overall mood and holiday spirit. I think that hearing the choir, the Liturgy of the Word, and praying with the rest of the congregation really brought home the notion that Christmas is here.

Family

There really is nothing like spending the holidays with your family: it really reinforces the importance of tradition. After Mass we drove down to my parents house in Millersville, MD, which is approximately 30-minutes from the Basilica in light traffic. We were greeted there by my parents, sister, brother-in-law, aunt, uncle, and cousins. Bing Crosby brought the holiday cheer, my mother made a fantastic dinner, and for a time I forgot all of the cares of my everyday life.

Christmas Eve at my parents has been a tradition as long as I can remember (before it was my parents’ house it was my grandparents, but still the same tradition in the same home). It’s pretty crazy to think about how memorable Christmas Eve really is: I can’t rememeber any other day of the year—not even Christmas—with the same level of detail over the same number of years.

Christmas Day, as our family tradition would have it, was spent with Christy’s family. In the morning, starting around 9:00am, we open presents with her parents and siblings at my mother-in-law’s house in Crownsville. Just like visiting my parents on Christmas Eve, Christmas Morning at my in-laws has become such an integral part of my Christmas tradition I can’t imagine doing it any other way. We eat a late breakfast (late for us since we’ve already been up a few hours), open gifts, then all travel to Christy’s Grandmother’s house to vsit with the extended family for the day. I love it, at least in part, because it allows me experience that thing I was always missing: the big family.

A Word About Oliver

This is, of course, Oliver’s first Christmas and all of the things one would expect to experience have come his way. Everyone wanted a photo with the little-guy, he got more presents than he could ever use, and all-the-while he had no idea anything notable was even happening.

For me, my first Christmas as a dad was a real treat that I’ll remember my whole life (even if he didn’t think anything of it).
