Sharing BEAG Ideas

When I started freelancing in 2013, I didn’t have all the answers. I still don’t. But I found that sharing my ideas, hopes, and fears with others on my blog helped me to refine them, and ultimately I am better for having done that.

When I founded The BEAG Co. earlier this year, I had planned to continue that practice of sharing knowledge and (half-formed) ideas on my blog — and, indeed, encouraging my growing team to do so as well. But it just didn’t happen that way. As the company grew, I found I had less and less time to write (or at least I made less time for it). And with it now being a company blog — not just my personal space — I felt like the bar was much higher. So high, I stopped trying to reach for it.

But the truth is that ideas don’t need to be fully formed to be worth sharing, and I want to lead a team that’s comfortable — even eager — to come forward with outside the box thoughts. I want us to participate in the larger political strategy, tech, and design communities. And I want us to be unafraid to go out on a limb, try something new, and learn from our mistakes. Because without taking risks there are no rewards.

And so, encouraged by Ev Williams’ Inside Medium, I’m opening up a new publication on Medium called BEAG Ideas — a place for out-loud thinking and conversation starting. I’m setting the bar intentionally low, because here the goal is to foster curiosity and discussion. Posts will be raw, they will be half-formed, etc. And that’s great.

“BEAG Ideas” will come with a sister publication — Digital+Field — where we’ll explore the shared future of digital and field strategy in politics in greater detail (and a bit more polish). I hope you’ll consider subscribing to them both.
