Think, Then Tweet

So you’d like to engage with your parishioners online. Great. Now where should you start?

As is true in most of life, in digital organizing poor prior planning precipitates piss poor performance. So have plan. Be intentional with your choices.

Before you go willy-nilly creating a new Twitter account or Facebook Page for your parish, ask yourself a few simple questions:

  1. What offline action am I aiming to encourage or facilitate with this online content? What goal will it help me to achieve?
  2. What is my ideal audience for the content I’ll be sharing?
  3. How will my ideal audience discover my content in the first place?
  4. Am I in a position to make a long term commitment to growing this digital community? (Digital organizing is a marathon, not a sprint.)

Your answers to these basic questions don’t need to be complicated. In fact, they shouldn’t be. But they should be crystal clear in your head before you begin.
