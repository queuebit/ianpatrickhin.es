Root For The Home Team

I’m an Orioles fan from Maryland living in Los Angeles. And I don’t mean a casual fan of the game, either. I’m a dyed-in-the-wool citizen of Birdland.

Since becoming a serious fan in 2005, I’ve watched most of their games. And let’s be clear: they’ve lost most of them. Nearly every Orioles team I’ve ever rooted for has sucked — that is, until last year.

In 2012, the O’s got hot. Red hot. In their first winning season since 1997, they were perhaps the most impressive team in the American League: 29-9 in one-run games; 16-2 in extra innings; and 74-0 when leading the game after 7 innings. They finished the season 2nd in the American League East and headed for the playoffs. And I largely missed it.

I’ve been to Camden Yards more often than I can count, but last Fall I watched from afar as my friends updated their Instagram feeds and Facebook posts with photos of the playoff atmosphere. I wasn’t with them; I was moving to Houston, Texas. It was like a cruel joke: years of watching horrible teams lose, and when the good team finally comes along I’m left out of the fun.

That was last year. Now, I’m in sunny Los Angeles, California, and I’m still watching nearly every game (courtesy of MLB.tv). My O’s are still playing well — 65-54 as of this writing — but I haven’t set foot in Baltimore in nearly a year. And because I’m in Pacific Time the games usually start at 4:00pm. It’s weird.

I’ve only been to two baseball games this year — both of them in Anaheim. I wasn’t expecting it, but it was actually pretty fun to watch the O’s play on the road. I felt a bit brash wearing all my gear in Angel Stadium, and it made my heart soar to see all the folks from The West Wing LA out in center field. (Those folks are awesome.)

If anything, living in LA has made me more of an Orioles fan. Now it’s not just about rooting for the home team, it’s about connecting with my home town. It’s really made me realize that you can take the guy out of Baltimore — and even drop him in sunny Southern California — but you can’t take the Baltimore out of the guy.

Let’s Go O’s!
