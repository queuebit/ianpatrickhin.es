How to easily ensure that you’re using a secure (SSL-encrypted) donation page

When Texas Senator Ted Cruz announced this week that he would seek the Republican presidential nomination, the national conversation quickly shifted to troubles with his new campaign website. Specifically, pundits quickly — and rightly — pointed out that Cruz’s donation page was not properly secured. This left Cruz’s donors vulnerable to what is known as a “man-in-the-middle” attack, in which a hacker impersonates one of the endpoints in the transaction and steals a donor’s credit card information.

Now the truth is that the deficiencies with Senator Cruz’s website are incredibly common — particularly in political campaign websites. Here’s how it happens:

  • an organization builds their website using a popular content management system (NationBuilder, WordPress, etc.);
  • then, they choose a payment processor (like Piryx, for example) with an “embeddable widget” that can be inserted as an iFrame on any page; and
  • They insert that “widget” on a non-secured page on their site.

The result is a “secure” donation form embedded within an “insecure” page on your site, making your donors susceptible to the aforementioned “man-in-the-middle” attack. It’s a bad, easily avoidable mistake that you shouldn’t fall victim to.

So what should you do instead?

  1. If you’re using NationBuilder, you should absolutely use their built-in donation page functionality. Every NationBuilder website supports a fully-functional, SSL-encrypted donation page template that’ll allow you to raise money while completely avoiding this problem. And you can connect that donation page to any of at least seven different payment processors.

  2. But what’s even better is that — unlike most third-party donation “widgets” — NationBuilder’s templates are totally customizable. You can make a NationBuilder donation page look like just about anything you can imagine: a multi-step form, all on one page (one or two columns), add custom fields, etc. The options are pretty much limitless, and the result is much more control over your donors experience than you’d get with a third-party tool.

  3. If you’re not using NationBuilder, or if you’re intent on using a third-party donation tool, just don’t embed the donation form on your site. Link to it. This is a common approach on the Democratic side with campaigns / organizations fundraising through ActBlue or NGP VAN, and a perfectly valid way to handle your online donations.

The bottom line is this: take the time to make sure you’re using an SSL-encrypted donation page. And if you’re not sure whether you are, or how to do it, just ask a professional. This is something that won’t cost you any money, but could cost you donations (and bad press!), so you want to make sure you do right from Day One.
