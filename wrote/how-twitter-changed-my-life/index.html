How Twitter changed my life

Someone once told me that Facebook is where you maintain weak ties with the people you know, but Twitter is where you develop strong ties with the people you wish you knew. That’s been my experience, and I think it’s part of the reason that I love Twitter so much.

Now, I don’t follow many people on Twitter. And I certainly don’t follow everyone I know offline. Instead, I follow people who I admire. The people who I aspire to be compared to by others.

That feels strange to read, even as I type it, but it’s the truth. By and large, the people I follow on Twitter comprise a list of people who I admire the most, and who (I believe) drive me to be a better person. And being a part of that group — rather than just a spectator — is the goalpost that I’m working towards.

So what makes someone a part of that group? What am I looking for? Well, I typically (and without any formal process) ask myself a few questions before I follow someone, such as:

  • Is this person a creator?
  • Do I like what they create? Is it the sort of thing that (a) I would want to use and (b) that I wish I had created?
  • Do they seem nice? (No one wants to follow a jerk.)
  • Do they seem authentic?
  • Do other people I respect follow them? Put another way, do they have the respect of people that I respect?

I think it’s largely because I’ve approached Twitter in this way that it’s had such a significant impact on my life. It’s given me a community that is bound by common interest and values — irrespective of geography — and I’m incredibly thankful for it. I can say honestly that I would not be the person that I am today without it.
