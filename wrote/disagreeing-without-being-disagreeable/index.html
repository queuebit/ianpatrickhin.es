Disagreeing Without Being Disagreeable

People can reasonably differ in opinion on a great range of topics. But there’s never a good justification for being discourteous.

Put another way: character is demonstrated by how you behave when no one is watching. Even more so when the entire Internet is watching.

Everyone needs to vent from time to time — and our first thoughts aren’t always reflective of our better angels — but it’s a good idea not to hit “publish” when you’re on a self-described “rant.” It just makes you look bad, and undermines the very point you’re trying to make.

A simple way to remind yourself of this is a short — but memorable and apropos — lesson my father tried for years to teach me: “Think. Then speak.”
