The "Project Overview" Document

Last Fall, my team began using Basecamp. Last month, it became the lynchpin that holds everything together. The difference has been in one change: we adopted “Project Overview Documents.”

Context: My Office

Before I explain what the “Project Overview” is all about, I should probably spend a brief moment and describe my team. At the office, I lead a team of 11 (myself included) people responsible for the internal and external communications of a state government agency. At a macro level, our agency has more than 6,700 employees, 24 local offices and a projected FY2013 budget of $2.5 billion. At any given time we might have between 25–40 projects up in the air, and Basecamp has proven invaluable in keeping all the balls in the air and moving forward.

The Project Overview

As I said, the self-made feature at the heart of my team’s success with Basecamp has been the “Project Overview” document.

For each newly initiated project — and especially for projects delegated to my team from above, rather than initiated from within — the very first thing I create is the “Project Overview” document.

It is a short, but substantive, description of the scope and intent of the project. What is the end goal? Why are we doing this? How did it get started? Who is the project manager? Etc. By putting the answers to these predictable questions down at the beginning, we have found that we avert the mid-project crisis of forgetfulness.

The Project Manager

I’ve also found that specifying a Project Manager up-front dramatically improves accountability. By default, Basecamp treats all participants in a project more-or-less equally, but in reality that’s not typically accurate. In reality, there are always people who are more accountable than others, and the distinction between the two is far from insignificant.

In my team, the Project Manager is the person who is fundamentally responsible for making sure that deadlines are met & that no project ever stalls. That’s a huge responsibility, especially since — with 30+ projects active at any given time — I’m not in a position to stay abreast of all of our projects at all times.

Wherein I Wrap It Up

“It’s a dangerous business, Frodo, going out your door. You step onto the road, and if you don’t keep your feet, there’s no knowing where you might be swept off to.”

— J.R.R. Tolkien, The Lord of the Rings

At its core, Basecamp’s value-added is that it clears up the ambiguity that so often slows down and/or kills complex projects. It keeps us all one “one page,” reduces the time we spend on inter-office coordination and thereby frees us up to create.

For us, the “Project Overview” is a common-sense addition to Basecamp’s already fantastic toolkit. It helps us — as Tolkien would say — to “keep our feet.”
