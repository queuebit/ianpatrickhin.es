Father's Day

Depending on your definition of when life truly begins, this is my first Father’s Day as a father. My son will be born on or around September 14 of this year, and with less than three months to go it feels like it’s right around the corner.

My wife and I have been chipping away at the preparation weekend after weekend, trying to transform our little row-house from a “perfect for two” loft to a home for a family. It’s been hard because our house is only about twelve feet wide and I don’t think the floor plan was really made with children in mind, but I think that everything is finally starting to come together. We’ve gotten most of the major furniture set up, and have reserved space for what we don’t have.

The best part about the process has been how exciting it feels when you finally get something all put together. The picture above is of his clothing hanging up in the wardrobe, sorted left-to-right by size. We had been collecting the clothes for some time (partly stuff we bought, but mostly from gifts) without much excitement, but when we hung them all up it really came together. The cutest thing so far is that little Tweed jacket we got him to wear this Thanksgiving (it’s even got arm patches!).
