Away We Go

I watch a lot of movies. I suppose I can thank my wife and Netflix for that. Normally they are entertaining; occasionally they are boring. Every so often, however, I find a real gem: a film whose characters, plot, and subtext seem to dovetail with my life so perfectly it is as if it was written especially for me. “Away We Go” was one of these movies.

It’s really not a complicated story: a thirty-something expectant couple travels the continent (USA & Canada) in search of a new home after the father’s parents announce that they’re leaving to move to Belgium. It’s romantic, funny, and quaint. But more importantly it’s incredibly similar to my life right now.

Aside from the oddly unsettling beard/hair/glasses similarities between myself and the main character, Burt, the film parallels my life in a number of ways. My son, Oliver, is approaching his first birthday, so I just recently went through the “expectant parent” thing. I’ve long been stuck with the relocation bug. And my parents, just this past Thursday, announced that they’d be moving from Maryland to Texas before the Winter.

Like the couple in the film, the thought of my parents moving to a place so inaccessibly distant threw my world into a tailspin. My wife and I have always talked, wistfully, about moving away to New York, Phoenix, Montréal, or, most longingly, Scotland. There were really only two factors keeping me rooted in Maryland: money and family. But that’s all changed, now.

With the family no longer being an issue, is money enough to keep me rooted? Probably not.

Of course, it isn’t as though I don’t like where I am. Baltimore is a fantastic place to live, with its tight-knit communities and local culture. Besides, it’s home. But if home, as they say, is where the heart is then a big part of my home–my family–is about to leave. The other part–my wife and son–will be with me wherever I am. I suppose, like it did for Burt, home has become a moveable feast.

So now that I know I’m looking for something, I just have to decide what I’m looking for.

Then “away we go.”
