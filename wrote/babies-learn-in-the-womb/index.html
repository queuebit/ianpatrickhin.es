Babies learn in the womb

As if we needed more evidence that babies are people from conception — not birth — a new study published in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences shows that an unborn baby can “can detect and remember discrete words” in utero, then recognize them after birth.

I’d always been told that unborn children learn their parents voices and can recognize them by sound after birth. The idea that specific words or phrases can be learned before birth, however, is news to me (though I can’t say I am surprised).

As a parent, my takeaway from this is: talk to your unborn kids during pregnancy. They hear you — and they probably appreciate the company.
