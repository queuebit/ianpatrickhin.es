Real Friendships, Online

There’s a conventional wisdom that someone can be an online friend, or they can be a “real” friend. As if people who you meet online and with whom you develop relationships over time aren’t really your friends. Well, that’s just rubbish.

My friend Jasmin Wong and I met in 2003–2004 when I first started blogging. We’ve stayed in touch since, and for the last two years she’s been thoughtful enough to send me Christmas cards all the way from Australia.

Embarrassingly, I have not returned the favor. Thoughtful has never been one of my hallmarks. But the very fact that I have a friendship—one of the longest lasting ones I can currently count—made and sustained entirely over the Internet is a testament to the power of the web to connect people.

So here’s my advice to you as we approach the new year: be yourself online. Say hello to that writer you’ve been reading silently for months. Write someone an email and introduce yourself. You never know: they may become a good friend. They are just a person, after all—just like you.
