Apple acquires Research In Motion,  Announces new ‘AppleBerry’ Smartphone

AppleBerry features A6 chip, selectable Siri voice, physical keyboard & user-replaceable battery

Cupertino, California (September 12, 2012) – Apple® today announced the acquisition of Research In Motion®, for $4.5 billion USD subject to US and Canadian regulatory approval. The Ontario based company, which has been a pioneer in mobile device development for nearly three decades, will become part of Apple’s iOS software division, where it will lead the development of the new AppleBerry™ — the most amazing Apple device yet. The AppleBerry will be packed with incredible new features never before seen in Apple mobile devices, including the new quad–core A6 processor for hyper fast performance and extraordinary graphics; new, selectable voice options for Siri™, the intelligent assistant that helps you get things done just by asking; a physical QWERTY keyboard that will allow accurate, eyes-free typing; and a user-replaceable battery.

“I’m happy to announce a tentative deal to acquire Research In Motion, which has long been one of the world’s premiere tech companies in the mobile space,” said Tim Cook, CEO of Apple. “With nearly 30-years of experience in the tech industry, and a record of developing some of the world’s most popular enterprise devices, I know that RIM will be a great addition to Apple’s mobile products division.”

The AppleBerry will come with the all new Berry iOS, the world’s most advanced operating system, which will include more than 250 new features including user-selectable voice options for Siri; Push-To-Talk™, enabling immediate walkie-talkie like communications between any two iMessage enabled devices; FaceTime™ VoiceMail, the world’s first-ever video voicemail system; email auto-responders; and user-selectable on-screen widgets. Berry iOS will also be available to all iPhone 4 and iPhone 4S customers, allowing them to experience these extraordinary new features on their existing devices.

“Apple plus BlackBerry equals AppleBerry, a huge breakthrough that will give millions of customers worldwide the best smartphone ever,” said Scott Forstal, Apple’s senior vice president of iOS software. “Powered by the all-new Berry iOS, and driven by RIM’s renowned QWERTY Keyboard, the AppleBerry will bring the best of both worlds to mobile computing.”

The AppleBerry and Berry iOS, will roll out worldwide to 22 more countries by January 2013, including Austria, Belgium, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Mexico, Netherlands, Norway, Singapore, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden and Switzerland. At that time, the iPhone 3GS, iPhone 4, iPhone 4S and iOS — known hereafter as iOS classic — will be discontinued.

For more information, visit the Apple.com.
