Struggling with time & attention

You may have noticed that the pace of my writing has slowed. Let me tell you why.

On February 17th I was diagnosed with adult attention deficit hyperactivity disorder. Specifically the inattentive-type, which is to say my struggle is with focusing rather than with hyperactivity. This marked the culmination of years of prodding by family and friends who, rightly, recognized that I was struggling with distractibility, procrastination, organization, and prioritization.

For most of my life I got by, with much success, by simply “winging it”: I didn’t study; I rarely did homework that wasn’t graded; and I frequently pulled late nights in order to complete projects on time (because I waited until the last minute). I was constantly behind on nearly everything—often because I had entirely forgotten that it needed to be done.

Of course, you never forget the top thing on your mind, and for me that was the website. I love writing for this site, and would regularly find that I’d be doing so at the direct expense of other, more important things—like spending time with my wife and son. This misplaced focus slowly—but steadily—began to levy a real toll on my relationships and happiness.

Something had to give.

Then, on February 7th, something clicked. I was driving back from a press conference and listening to “Back to Work” with Merlin Mann and Dan Benjamin when Merlin began to tell the story of how he, as an adult, was diagnosed with and began treatment for ADHD. Merlin Mann—Mr. Productivity—had attention deficit disorder. My harsh, inaccurate stereotypes were instantly shattered.

If Merlin—someone I really look up to in terms of personal achievement, self-management, and quality of work—was struggling with this problem then maybe, just maybe, I might be too. So I asked my doctor about it at my next physical exam, which was only 10 days later.

Sure enough, I had every symptom on the list. In fact, my doctor speculated that my over-the-top caffeine habit (5–8 mugs of coffee/tea per day) was a form of self-treatment—that I had been using the caffeine to help restore balance and focus. I began formalized treatment just prior to departing for Ireland in March, and began to see a real difference once I returned to my routine about 10-days later.

The month that followed has been one of the best of my adult life. I feel like I am in command of my day—of myself—in a way that’s difficult to describe. For the first time in memory I have the confidence of knowing that I’m focused on the right things at the right time. I’m paradoxically working harder than I ever have before, yet not at all overwhelmed or exhausted by it. It’s amazing.

So what does this have to do with my lack of writing on the site? (Don’t worry, I haven’t lost focus!) The reason I have slowed on the writing is because, well, it’s not my top priority right now. It is a priority, but not the top one. Nor the second. In fact, it’s fourth.

I have lots of tasks (more than 1,300 currently in OmniFocus) for which I am responsible, but only a few real priorities. They are to:

  1. Love and serve the Lord in accordance with the teaching of the Church;
  2. Be an exceptional father to my son and husband to my wife;
  3. Excel at my work in order to earn a living (see priority #2) and serve my fellow citizens; and
  4. Pursue my personal passions and interests.

That’s it. Those are my priorities. At any given moment I may have 30 tasks that need completing, 12 of which are past due, and only 30 minutes in which to work. How do I decide what to do next? I work down the list in order of priority: God; Family; Work; and Personal.

In practice, that means that the personal goals and projects come last, and therefore are often deferred. It’s irresponsible of me to devote time and energy to writing for this website when I haven’t said my prayers, cleaned the house, played with my son, or dealt with work.

I love writing, and I am humbled and honored that each and every one of you has chosen to give me your invaluable time and attention. That’s why I can’t imagine myself giving this site up: it means far too much to me.

My hope is that a slower pace will mean that what I do post—both link posts and original pieces—will be a bit more thoughtfully curated and written. There are lots of articles and the like that seem perfect for a link post in the moment, but decidedly less so with a day or so’s distance. Expect to see less of that.

The bottom line is that I’ve been writing less because I’ve been getting more of the important things done. Perhaps I’ll flesh the whats and hows of that out a bit over the coming weeks. In the meantime, thanks again for your time and attention—and for your patience as I work to regain control of mine.
