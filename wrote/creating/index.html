Creating

I am a generalist — which is to say that I know a little bit about a lot of things. This has innumerable advantages, most notably that it allows me to add value to a diverse range of projects in lots of different ways. But while I’ve always found it easy to be a contributor, I’ve found it incredibly difficult to be a creator.

Creators have cultivated within themselves that special skillset that allows them to see the world as it isn’t — yet — and make their imagination come to life. And I find myself incredibly jealous of them for it. But the truth is that the only difference between creators and non-creators is their mindset and their work ethic, and both of those things are within my power to change about myself.

To my mind, creating is simply the act of conceiving of something new and bringing it into existence. That new thing does not need to be artistic. It could be a new piece of public policy, a commit on Github, a new perspective or a post on your blog. If you make something new that comes from your heart, you’re a creator. And creators change the world.

A friend recently shared the following quote with me, which has really taken root in my mind:

When you don’t create things, you become defined by your tastes rather than ability. Your tastes only narrow and exclude people. So create.

—Why the Lucky Stiff

I want people to know me for the value I add to my community, not for what I’m interested in. And I mean that in a broad way — not in terms of the typical concept of “community activism,” but from a perspective of whether the things I’ve created have added value to the people around me. If you stop and reflect for a moment, I suspect you’ll find that that is the common thread that unites all the people you admire the most: they’ve created something that enriches the lives of the people around them.

So… without making bold predictions, I’ll say that I’m going to work harder at being creative (in the literal sense). I’ll no longer let my self-doubt be the place where ideas go to die. When I have an idea, I’ll try it — or at least share it — even if doing so means that I’ll need to learn new skills necessary for bringing it into fruition.

At a most basic level, that probably means more regular writing here. Blogging, after all, is creating.
